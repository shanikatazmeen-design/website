Chineese cuisine

Chinese cuisine is an incredibly diverse, ancient culinary tradition defined by immense regional variety, with staples like rice in the south, wheat-based noodles/buns in the north, and a focus on stir-frying, steaming, and braising. Renowned for balancing color, aroma, and taste, it often uses ingredients like soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and sesame oil to create iconic dishes such as Dim Sum, Peking Duck, and Hot Pot.
